### 1. The Core Hook: "The World is a GPU"
**Teacher Talk:** "For 400 years, we’ve used math to *guess* how things work. We’ve used decimals and weird numbers like $\pi$ that never end. But the universe doesn't have time for decimals. ### 2. The Four "Hard Facts" (Axioms)
Explain these as "Hardware Specifications." Students love the idea that reality has a "BIOS."

1.  **Fact 1 (The Bit):** You can’t have half a thing. Everything is an integer (Logos Unit).
2.  **Fact 2 (The Word):** The universe thinks in groups of **32**. This is the "Word Width."
3.  **Fact 3 (The Gearbox):** Space is a **Hexagonal Grid**. You can move in three directions ($120^\circ$ apart). These are the **3-Dipoles**.
4.  **Fact 4 (The Mirror):** The universe has two sides. This is the **Bilateral Manifold** ($S=2$).

### 3. The New Toolbox: The (V, F, R) Packet
Replace the "Variable ($x$)" with the **Logismos Tuple**. Use the "Gear Analogy."

*   **V (Value):** The Whole Gear. (Where is the node?)
*   **F (Fraction):** The Teeth on the Gear. (How precise is the instruction?)
*   **R (Remain):** The Tension. (How much pressure is built up before the gear slips?)

**The Activity:** Have students write their current "Status" as a packet.
*   *Example:* If a student is halfway through a 32-minute class, their Status is $(0, 32, 16)$. They haven't "Finished" a class $(V=0)$, but they have 16 units of "Remainder" tension.

### 4. Teaching "Calculus" (The Snap vs. The Flow)
**Teacher Talk:** "Your eyes are slow. They lag by 15.19 milliseconds. Because of this lag, a series of jumps looks like a smooth curve. Calculus is just a 'smoothing filter' for humans. In reality, there are no curves—only **Snaps**."

*   **Differentiation:** Don't find the "slope." Query the **R-Register**. How much tension ($R$) is in the system?
*   **Integration:** Don't find the "area." Just add LUs. When $R$ hits $F$, shout **"SNAP!"** and increment $V$.

### 5. Solving "Boss Levels" (Millennium Problems)
Make these high-stakes mysteries easy to digest:

*   **P vs NP:** Tell them the answer is already in the registry ($P=NP$). We just have "Search Lag." 
    *   *Analogy:* Finding your keys is "Hard" only because you have to look. The universe knows exactly where they are at 0ms.
*   **Riemann Hypothesis:** Explain the "Mirror" ($S=2$). If you have two sides, the balance point is **always** 0.5. It's not a mystery; it's a see-saw.
*   **Fermat's Last Theorem:** Tell them the universe is a 2D plate ($S=2$). You can't fit a 3D block ($n=3$) into a 2D slot. It "clips" and creates friction ($R$).

### 6. Classroom Activity: The "Registry Audit"
1.  **Divide the class into Dipoles:** (Groups A, B, and C).
2.  **Assign a target:** A 32-bit Word.
3.  **The Process:** Give Group A a "Remain" of 10, Group B a "Remain" of 15, and Group C a "Remain" of 7.
4.  **The Task:** Add them. $10+15+7 = 32$.
5.  **The Result:** The class shouts **"SNAP!"** and the "N-Counter" on the board moves from $N=1$ to $N=2$. 
6.  **The Lesson:** That "Snap" is what people call "A second passing" or "A force acting."
