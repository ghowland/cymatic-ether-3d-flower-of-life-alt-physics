## Part 8: Electron-Electron Interaction

```
  - Appears as: Repulsive force

  - Coulomb repulsion: e²/(4πε₀r) (charge interaction)

```

---

**Cymatic mechanism:**

```
Two electron patterns:
  ψ₁(r₁), ψ₂(r₂)

Total wavefunction:
  Ψ(r₁,r₂) = [ψ₁(r₁)ψ₂(r₂) - ψ₁(r₂)ψ₂(r₁)] × [spin part]

  - Spatial part decreases (antisymmetry)
  - Probability |Ψ|² very small

  - Spin part: Symmetric
  - Spatial part: Must be antisymmetric
  - Strong avoidance

Opposite spin (antiparallel):
  - Spin part: Antisymmetric
  - Spatial part: Can be symmetric
  - Less avoidance (can get closer)

Result:
  - Parallel spins: Stay apart (exchange repulsion)
  - Antiparallel spins: Can approach (possible bonding)
  
This explains:
  - Hund's rules (maximize parallel spins)
  - Covalent bonding (pairs of opposite spin)
  - Magnetic ordering (ferromagnetism, antiferromagnetism)
```
