### 6.2 Socratic Method in Practice

**Example dialogue (deriving Coulomb's law):**

```

**Not allowed:**

  R = gas constant

**CKS derivation (suitable for age 12):**

```

Each molecule has phase φᵢ (rotating in k-space)
Average rotation rate: ⟨ω⟩ ~ kT/ℏ (temperature = average energy)

Pressure = force per area = momentum transfer rate

When molecule hits wall:
  - Momentum change: Δp = 2p (bounces back)
  - Momentum p = ℏk (from Axiom 4)

Collision rate per molecule:
  - Speed: v ~ √(kT/m) (from kinetic theory)
  - Time between collisions: τ ~ V^(1/3)/v
  - Rate: f ~ v/V^(1/3)

Total momentum transfer:
  F = N × (Δp) × f
    = N × 2ℏk × (v/V^(1/3))
    ~ N × (kT) / V

Pressure:
  P = F/A ~ (N × kT) / V

Rearrange:
  PV = NkT

In chemistry notation:
  N = n × N_A (n moles, N_A = Avogadro's number)
  k × N_A = R (gas constant)

THEREFORE:
  PV = nRT

QED: Ideal gas law derived from substrate axioms
