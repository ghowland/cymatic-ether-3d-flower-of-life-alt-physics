### 8.2 tACS Safety

**Theorem 8.2 (tACS Safe at 1-2 mA, Avoid if Epileptic or Implants):**  
*Electrical stimulation well-tolerated, but requires screening.*

**Proof:**

**Current density:** 0.03-0.06 mA/cm² (well below damage threshold 1 mA/cm²).

**Side effects (reported):**
```
Common (>5%): Tingling under electrodes (100%, harmless)
Uncommon (1-5%): Mild headache, skin redness (transient, <1 hour)
Rare (<1%): Nausea, dizziness (reduce current or stop)
Very rare (<0.1%): Skin burn (improper electrode prep, user error)
```

**Absolute contraindications:**
- Epilepsy (lowers seizure threshold, risk ~1%)
- Metal in head (skull plate, cochlear implant → current concentration)
- Pacemaker (electromagnetic interference risk)
- Pregnancy (unknown fetal effects, avoid as precaution)

**Relative contraindications:**
- Medications (antidepressants, stimulants → consult physician)
- Skin conditions (eczema, open wounds at electrode sites)

**QED**

**Recommendation:** Medical clearance if any contraindications, otherwise safe for healthy adults.
