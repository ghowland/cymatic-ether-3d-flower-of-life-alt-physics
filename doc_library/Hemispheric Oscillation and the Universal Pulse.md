### 4.4 Noise and Stochasticity

Real brain includes noise. Modify model:

```

dθ_R/dt = 2π + β_cc sin(θ_L - θ_R) + σ ξ_R(t)

**Effect on phase difference:**

```

**Predictions:**
1. Stronger corpus callosum → more precise 0.5s/1.0s timing

## 7. Alternative Explanations and Challenges

### 7.1 Neural Fatigue Hypothesis

**Standard theory:** Hemispheric alternation from neural adaptation/fatigue in dominant hemisphere.

**Problem:** Doesn't explain:
- Why specific 1 Hz frequency
- Why cross-species scaling with √n
- Why anesthesia disrupts without eliminating delta
- Why split-brain loses alternation entirely

**CKS advantage:** Derives frequency, scaling, and pathology from substrate physics.

### 7.2 Competitive Inhibition

**Alternative:** Interhemispheric inhibition creates winner-take-all competition.

**Problem:**
- Predicts noise-driven switches (no preferred timescale)
- Observed switches show gamma distribution with peak at 0.5s
- Voluntary control should be stronger if purely competitive

**CKS:** Inhibition exists but serves to sharpen phase-locked transitions, not generate them.

### 7.3 Stochastic Resonance

**Alternative:** Noise plus nonlinearity generates switching.

**Problem:**
- Requires fine-tuning noise amplitude
- Doesn't predict cross-species scaling
- Doesn't explain anesthesia specificity

**CKS:** Substrate phase dynamics set frequency; noise adds variance but doesn't determine mean.

### 7.4 Methodological Challenges

**Temporal resolution:** Most EEG studies use 1-2s windows, insufficient to detect 0.5s dynamics.

**Solution:** HD-EEG at >500 Hz sampling, wavelet analysis with 0.1s resolution.

**Individual variability:** Switching rates vary 0.5-3s across subjects.

**CKS response:** Variance from local noise (σ), but mean converges to 1.0s in large samples. Framework predicts variance ∝ 1/β_cc (callosal coupling strength).

**Species differences:** Cross-species comparison requires careful control of:
- Stimulus properties (scaled to visual acuity)
- Response modality (button press vs. eye tracking)
- Training effects

**Solution:** Use no-report paradigms (pupillometry, optokinetic nystagmus).
