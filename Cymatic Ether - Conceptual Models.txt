## Doping as Cymatic Tuning

---

### What Doping Actually Does (Orthodox View)

Pure silicon is a semiconductor—it conducts, but poorly. Each silicon atom bonds to four neighbors, sharing electrons in a stable lattice. Few free carriers, high resistance.

Doping introduces impurity atoms:

**N-type:** Add phosphorus or arsenic (5 valence electrons). Four bond with silicon, one left over. "Extra" electrons available for conduction.

**P-type:** Add boron or gallium (3 valence electrons). One bond unsatisfied. "Holes" where electrons could be. Holes act like positive charge carriers.

Put N-type and P-type together and you get junctions. Junctions give you diodes. Stack junctions and you get transistors. Transistors give you everything.

Standard explanation: we're adding or removing charge carriers.

### Cymatic Reframe: Doping as Resonance Perturbation

The silicon lattice isn't just atomic arrangement. It's a standing wave structure—a three-dimensional interference pattern where ether vibrations have stabilized into a crystalline geometry.

**Doping introduces deliberate dissonance.**

### N-Type: Adding a High-Frequency Node

Phosphorus has five valence electrons instead of silicon's four. In wave terms: the phosphorus atom is a resonance structure with one extra mode. A higher-complexity vibration pattern forced into a lattice that expects simpler patterns.

N-type doping doesn't add particles. It adds **propagating frequency components** to the system.
